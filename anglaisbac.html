I. Grammar Essentials All Series

Here are the three pillars of the English exams at the BAC.

1. The Passive Voice

Focus on the action, not the doer.

Formula: To Be + Past Participle

Active: The government builds new roads.
Passive: New roads are built by the government.
2. Conditional Sentences (If)

Crucial for expressing hypothesis.

Type 2 (Unreal Present):
If I had money, I would buy a car.

Type 3 (Regret/Past):
If I had studied, I would have passed.
3. Reported Speech (Indirect Speech)

When you report what someone said, you generally move one tense back (Backshift).

Direct: "I am busy today," he said.
Indirect: He said (that) he was busy that day.

üìù Mock Exam Practice

A. Rewrite the sentences as indicated:

  1. "Did you finish your homework?" the teacher asked me.
    The teacher asked me if...
  2. People speak English all over the world.
    English...
  3. I didn't wake up early, so I missed the bus.
    If I...

Answers:
1. The teacher asked me if I had finished my homework.
2. English is spoken all over the world.
3. If I had woken up early, I would not have missed the bus.
